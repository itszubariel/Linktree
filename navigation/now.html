What I'm Doing Now.

March '26

March has mostly been about finishing two collaboration projects that carried over from February. Working with friends has made the process a lot more enjoyable and keeps things moving instead of letting projects sit unfinished. The plan is to ship both before the end of the month.

I finally finished Red Dead Redemption 2 after avoiding it for years because I had Arthur’s death spoiled early on. Even knowing what was coming didn’t stop the ending from hitting hard. I’ve also watched a couple movies recently that have stuck with me way longer than expected.

Gaming-wise I’ve been spending a lot of time on MCSR Ranked and currently sit at Gold 3. The same-seed head-to-head format makes it ridiculously competitive and addictive, probably the most fun I’ve had playing Minecraft in years.

Gym has also stayed consistent. I’m running a push/pull/legs split around 5–6 days a week and it’s become the perfect reset after long coding sessions.

Earlier Months

February '26

February was fairly relaxed but still productive. I started collaborating with a friend on finishing a few smaller projects that had been sitting around unfinished. I also got heavily into MCSR Ranked during this time and began climbing the ranks.

January '26

January was more of a creative and slower month. I read several books, watched a lot of great movies, and released my Minecraft mod HoL. It’s a single-player Lifesteal-style progression mod where dying removes a permanent heart and rare crafted items restore them. It ended up reaching around 1.2k downloads, which was a nice surprise.

December '25

December was mostly about experimenting with new ideas and cleaning up older projects. I spent a lot of time coding smaller tools and improving parts of my portfolio and other personal sites.

November '25

In November I spent a lot of time experimenting with backend development and building random utilities and small tools. Most of my learning now comes from just building things rather than following structured tutorials.

October '25

October was mostly about programming and experimenting with new ideas. I spent time working on personal projects, improving some older codebases, and generally exploring new technologies that caught my interest.
